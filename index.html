Championing the Red Sea: Education, Innovation, and Impact

In 2024, KAUST’s connection to the Red Sea deepened, reflecting an enduring commitment to aquatic stewardship—a principle embedded since the university’s founding. Rooted in its very genesis, this connection has evolved into a living relationship with the surrounding marine environment. Through education, community engagement, applied research, and cross-sector partnerships, KAUST continues to strengthen this bond, shaping a model of stewardship that integrates science, innovation, and societal impact.

Aligned with the goals of the UN Ocean Decade, these efforts extend beyond local waters to contribute to the global call for sustainable ocean management. The Red Sea, one of the world’s most biodiverse and resilient marine ecosystems, stands as both a national treasure and a vital proving ground for innovation. Its protection and sustainable use are central to the Kingdom’s transformation under Vision 2030—where ocean health underpins food security, climate resilience, and the growth of a vibrant blue economy. The following highlights showcase just a few of the ways in which KAUST is leading efforts to safeguard our oceans and advance a sustainable future for the Kingdom and the world.

Inspiring Stewardship: Education and Community Outreach

Each year, KAUST celebrates World Ocean Day with a program of activities that engage both the academic community and the public, underscoring the University’s commitment to fostering ocean awareness and sustainability. The 2024 program featured several activities, including a movie screening and a guided tour of KAUST’s marine research facilities, which play a central role in the University’s ongoing marine conservation work. A highlight of the program was the keynote by Professor Carlos M. Duarte, Distinguished Professor of Marine Science, delivered as part of Springer Nature’s SDG Talks global series marking World Ocean Day. In his talk, “Rebuilding Marine Life,” Professor Duarte shared an optimistic vision for restoring the ocean’s natural capital within one human generation. He emphasized the transformative potential of sustainable fisheries, aquaculture, and tourism in advancing regenerative blue economies—illustrating how science-based management and community awareness can align to sustain both livelihoods and biodiversity.

Extending awareness to national communities through creative collaborations with industry and civil society, KAUST researchers from the Marine Microbiomes Lab partnered with Albaik—the well-known Saudi food chain—to deliver an outreach event for the Jeddah community. The initiative raised awareness about the significance of the Red Sea, the environmental threats it faces, and the collective actions that can protect and conserve this vital ecosystem.

Building on this mission to make science more accessible to the public, KAUST researchers, including Professor Raquel Peixoto, were featured in “Beneath the Surface: The Fight for Corals,” a Warner Bros documentary produced in collaboration with Red Sea Global (the Kingdom’s largest developer of sustainable tourism destinations along the Red Sea coast), broadcast globally on Earth Day 2024. The film highlighted KAUST’s pioneering research on coral microbiome restoration and the University’s role in developing coral probiotics to enhance reef resilience. Through this international platform, KAUST shared the story of Red Sea science and innovation with millions of viewers worldwide—demonstrating how scientific excellence can inspire global action and foster a deeper public appreciation for ocean conservation.

Innovating for the Future: Research and Industry Partnerships

Beyond awareness, KAUST’s scientists are driving tangible solutions to protect and restore marine ecosystems under threat—translating research into real-world impact through collaborations with leading national and global partners. In partnership with the Saudi Red Sea Authority and Red Sea Global, KAUST researchers are co-developing conservation frameworks that embed sustainability into coastal development and regenerative tourism models. These collaborations bridge science, policy, and industry to ensure that economic progress reinforces, rather than compromises, ecosystem integrity.

KAUST is also pioneering new technologies for marine ecosystem restoration. Through the Building Coral initiative, KAUST and Mars Sustainable Solutions introduced the MARRS method to Saudi Arabia’s reefs, training KAUST researchers in Reef Star fabrication and installation and deploying 150 Reef Stars with more than 2,250 locally sourced coral fragments. This collaboration exemplified international knowledge transfer, as KAUST researchers learned and adapted the proven MARRS restoration system to local Red Sea conditions. Building on this foundation, the team integrated coral probiotic treatments on a subset of outplants to test enhanced thermal resilience—combining global restoration expertise with KAUST’s pioneering microbiome science to advance reef recovery under climate stress. Such engagements complement the KAUST Coral Restoration Initiative, the largest coral restoration project in the world at NEOM.

Widening KAUST’s marine conservation contributions to NEOM, KAUST experts have been directly involved in the Ras Baridi turtle conservation program, a NEOM-led initiative protecting one of the Red Sea’s most important nesting sites for endangered sea turtles. This fieldwork exemplifies the University’s hands-on approach to ecosystem preservation, combining advanced monitoring technologies with on-the-ground ecological management. Complementing this regional effort, the newly established KAUST–SHAMS Turtle Hospital—a collaboration with SHAMS, the General Authority for Coral Reefs and Turtles Conservation in the Red Sea—provides critical care and rehabilitation for injured sea turtles while serving as an educational hub for marine conservation. Part of the Blue KAUST initiative, the hospital strengthens the link between science, education, and community action, showcasing how research and local engagement can work together to protect iconic marine species and restore ecological balance along the Red Sea coast.

These applied initiatives are underpinned by world-leading scientific research. In Nature Communications, KAUST researchers co-authored a study on coral reefs as sentinels of global ecosystem change, revealing how these ecosystems both reflect and resist climate pressures. In Nature Climate Change, the publication “Marine megafauna act as trees in the ocean” uncovered the vital role of whales, sharks, and other large species in carbon storage and nutrient cycling—highlighting their indispensable contribution to planetary health.

From the reefs of the Red Sea to global research networks, KAUST continues to translate discovery into impact. By uniting education, innovation, and partnership, the University stands at the forefront of efforts to safeguard ocean life and enable a regenerative blue economy—where thriving ecosystems and human prosperity advance hand in hand.
